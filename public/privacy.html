Privacy Policy

stupidlist — A stupid simple daily task manager

Your data is yours. Period.

All your content is protected with end-to-end encryption. Your lists, tasks, and notes are encrypted before they leave your device. Nobody can read your data — not us, not anyone.

We do not sell, share, or monetize your data in any way. There is no tracking, no analytics profiling, and no third-party data sharing.

What we collect

When you sign in (optional), we collect:

  • Email address — Used solely for authentication. We use Google Sign-In and/or Apple Sign-In to verify your identity.
  • User ID — A unique identifier to associate your data with your account for cloud sync.
  • Your content — Tasks, lists, and projects you create are stored encrypted in the cloud so you can sync across devices.

If you use the app without signing in, no personal data is collected. Everything stays on your device.

How we use your data

Your data is used exclusively for app functionality:

  • Authenticating your account
  • Syncing your tasks across your devices
  • Enabling list sharing when you choose to share

We do not use your data for advertising, analytics, tracking, or any purpose other than making the app work for you.

Third-party services

We use the following services:

  • Firebase Authentication — For sign-in (Google, Apple)
  • Cloud Firestore — For encrypted data storage and sync

These services are provided by Google and are subject to Google's Privacy Policy.

Data retention

Your data is stored as long as you have an account. You can delete your data at any time by signing out and clearing app storage. To request complete data deletion, contact us.

Children's privacy

stupidlist is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page.
